# Signature Feature Patterns

Use this reference to choose a landing feature that makes the value visible before the lead form.

The feature can be a section inside a landing or the full landing model. When the ad promise is participatory, such as "calculate", "discover", "diagnose", "see your route", or "estimate your potential", prefer a sequential value exchange landing where the feature is the page and the final lead form comes after the result.

## Decision Matrix

Score each candidate from 1-5:

| Criterion | Question |
|---|---|
| Desire | Does it show the outcome the visitor wants? |
| Fear relief | Does it reduce the main hesitation? |
| Brand fit | Does it match the voice, palette, and promise? |
| Tech signal | Does it feel modern, interactive, or data-informed? |
| Credibility | Does it make the offer feel more real? |
| Actionability | Does it produce a natural next step? |
| Message match | Does it fulfill the ad/angle promise immediately? |
| Data value | Does every input produce visible value for the visitor? |
| Compliance | Can it avoid prohibited claims? |
| Performance | Can it stay fast and responsive on mobile? |
| Build speed | Can it be built with current app patterns? |

Choose one winner. If two features tie, prefer the one that lets the visitor participate.

## Patterns

### Guided Calculator Landing

Best for: savings, inflation, retirement, investment education, cost-of-inaction, and planning angles.

Experience: a 3-5 step flow asks for bucketed amounts, time horizon, goal, and comfort level. The UI reveals an educational scenario, range, or route before the final lead form.

Why it works: the visitor earns a useful result and understands why a follow-up call could help.

Guardrail: use assumptions, ranges, and educational framing. Do not promise gains or present the result as personalized advice.

CTA: "Recibir mi calculo", "Ver mi ruta educativa", "Quiero que revisen mi escenario".

### Potential Diagnostic Landing

Best for: broad finance, trading education, business growth, consultative offers, and high-consideration leads.

Experience: the visitor answers intent, current situation, timeline, and friction questions. The result gives a profile, readiness score, or next-step map.

Why it works: it turns vague interest into a qualified, call-ready reason to continue.

Guardrail: show the diagnostic as orientative and avoid ranking the visitor with shame or false urgency.

CTA: "Enviar mi diagnostico", "Ver mi siguiente paso", "Solicitar una revision".

### Qualification Quiz Landing

Best for: Meta traffic, course funnels, advisory calls, and offers where lead quality matters more than raw volume.

Experience: simple multiple-choice questions start with low-friction intent and end with contact capture after a result or eligibility-style explanation.

Why it works: it gathers zero-party data while making the form feel like the natural end of a guided process.

Guardrail: be clear that the next step is a human or educational follow-up. Do not imply guaranteed acceptance, approval, or profit.

CTA: "Comprobar mi perfil", "Enviar mis respuestas", "Quiero que me contacten".

### Gated Result Simulator

Best for: AI tools, trading systems, analytics products, and automated-strategy angles.

Experience: the visitor selects scenario inputs, sees partial preview states, and unlocks the complete educational interpretation at the final lead form.

Why it works: it demonstrates technology and creates a reason to submit without hiding all value.

Guardrail: never fabricate performance proof or profit curves. Use sample scenarios, confidence language, and visible limitations.

CTA: "Recibir el informe", "Enviar mi escenario", "Ver la interpretacion completa".

### Potential Map

Best for: savings, first investment, financial education, planning.

Experience: user enters or selects savings amount, horizon, and risk comfort. The UI reveals 2-4 possible routes with educational tradeoffs.

Why it works: turns money anxiety into a navigable map.

Guardrail: never present routes as advice or guaranteed return.

CTA: "Recibir mi mapa educativo", "Descargar mi ruta", "Ver mi siguiente paso".

### Inflation / Cost-of-Inaction Calculator

Best for: savings, retirement, cash management, investment education.

Experience: user sets amount and timeframe. The UI shows purchasing-power erosion or opportunity cost as an educational scenario.

Why it works: makes inaction visible without promising gains.

Guardrail: label assumptions clearly and avoid exact future claims.

CTA: "Calcular mis opciones", "Ver alternativas educativas".

### Risk Profile Simulator

Best for: first investment, wealth education, trading onboarding.

Experience: a short quiz produces a profile such as conservative, balanced, exploratory, or advanced.

Why it works: gives identity and direction while reducing fear.

Guardrail: say "perfil orientativo" and connect to learning paths, not personalized investment instructions.

CTA: "Descubrir mi perfil", "Recibir mi checklist".

### Market Context Cockpit

Best for: trading, crypto, commodities, market education, broker-like offers.

Experience: live ticker, symbol overview, heatmap, and a small explanation panel that teaches how to interpret context.

Why it works: real market data raises seriousness and perceived sophistication.

Guardrail: frame as context and education, not signals, alerts, or guaranteed entries.

CTA: "Acceder al bootcamp", "Recibir la clase inicial", "Explorar el metodo".

### Strategy Preview / Demo Lab

Best for: automated trading, AI systems, investment tools, analytics products.

Experience: simulated inputs show how a framework evaluates trend, volatility, risk, or exposure limits.

Why it works: makes the method feel tangible before asking for contact details.

Guardrail: use sample scenarios and do not show fabricated profit curves as proof.

CTA: "Ver la demo", "Recibir el informe".

### Comparison Lab

Best for: any offer where alternatives are confusing.

Experience: user toggles between "sin direccion" and "con plan", or compares routes by effort, risk, time, and learning required.

Why it works: clarifies why the offer exists.

Guardrail: keep comparisons factual and educational.

CTA: "Elegir mi ruta", "Descargar la guia".

### Progress / Readiness Scan

Best for: bootcamps, courses, SaaS onboarding, high-friction decisions.

Experience: checklist or quick diagnostic returns a readiness percentage and the missing steps.

Why it works: goal-gradient effect; users want to complete the route.

Guardrail: do not shame the user or create false urgency.

CTA: "Completar mi ruta", "Enviar mi diagnostico".

### AI Advisor Preview

Best for: AI-branded offers and guided education.

Experience: the UI asks 1-3 questions and returns a sample plan, explanation, or learning path.

Why it works: demonstrates intelligence and personalization.

Guardrail: call it an educational preview; avoid regulated advice or "the AI recommends buying X".

CTA: "Recibir mi plan educativo", "Hablar con un asesor educativo".

### Conversational Qualification

Best for: consultative offers, high-ticket services, Meta lead traffic, and audiences that need guidance before a form.

Experience: a short chat-like flow asks up to six easy questions, starting with low-friction intent questions and ending with contact capture or a lead form.

Why it works: creates a sense of human guidance and gathers zero-party data without presenting a long form upfront.

Guardrail: set expectations about who will follow up, on which channel, and when. Do not simulate a regulated adviser.

CTA: "Ver mi siguiente paso", "Enviar mi diagnostico", "Quiero que me contacten".

### Market Reality Check

Best for: finance education pages that need seriousness but should not become trading pages.

Experience: a small ticker, index chart, or macro indicator sits beside educational copy explaining volatility, inflation, or market cycles.

Why it works: gives the landing factual gravity without pretending to forecast the future.

Guardrail: keep the widget secondary to the user's learning path; do not label live data as a signal.

CTA: "Entender el contexto", "Recibir la guia", "Explorar con criterio".
